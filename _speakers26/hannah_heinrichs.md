I’m a PhD researcher in circadian neuroscience at the Max Planck Institute for Biological Cybernetics in Tübingen. Trained as a psychologist and neuroscientist, I study how sleep, light perception, and circadian rhythms interact in humans. I believe that understanding these mechanisms can help people make sense of their internal timing — especially in sleep-wake disorders and related mental‑health challenges.

Timing in the human body - mechanisms of the internal clock to sleep-wake behaviour

I study how two biological drivers, the circadian “body clock” and sleep pressure, work together to shape alertness, hormones, perception, and other bodily functions using tightly controlled protocols. By separating clock time from time awake, I can pinpoint what’s driven by the internal clock versus sleep habits.
